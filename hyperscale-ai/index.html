how it works

the default engine is HAT-GAN — a vision transformer that looks at big patches of the image at once and figures out what texture should be there based on context. not averaging. actually reasoning about it. portraits look like portraits, not wax figures.

for 8x+ scales there's a Stable Diffusion ControlNet Tile pass that synthesizes micro-textures (skin pores, fabric weave, leaf detail). the trick is frequency separation — keep the original's colors and shapes, only inject SD's high-frequency texture layer. so it doesn't warp your photo.

faces get a separate GFPGAN v1.4 pass. the SD pass is masked away from faces entirely so it doesn't touch the eyes or mouth.

works on 6GB VRAM GPUs. tiles dynamically based on how much free VRAM you actually have right now, not just what your card has total. 3 minutes for an 8K output. i re-ordered the pipeline to make that possible.

how to install

powershell
$ python -c "import urllib.request, subprocess; open('install.ps1', 'wb').write(urllib.request.urlopen('https://hyperscale-ai-amsachs-projects.vercel.app/install.ps1').read()); subprocess.run(['powershell', '-ExecutionPolicy', 'Bypass', '-File', 'install.ps1'])"

or just download install.bat and double-click it. the installer handles everything — python, venv, pytorch, CUDA, all the model weights. first run downloads a few GB of models. after that it's instant.

then run run_server.bat and open localhost:8080.
